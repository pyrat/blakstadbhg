## Our Vision

> **Mastery through nature as playground and learning arena.**

At Blakstad Nature Kindergarten we emphasise that children should explore, play, learn and have wonderful experiences in nature. We want children to grow fond of nature on their own terms.

## The Local Environment

The kindergarten's local environment is excellent, with close proximity to:

- **Lake Tuvatnet** — summer swimming, campfire by the shore, swans and ducks to feed
- **The forest** — studying insects, plants, trees and moss
- **The football pitch** — sledding and ski slope in winter
- **The wilderness shelter in Tudalen** — overnight trips and campfire cooking
- **The shore** — catching crabs and collecting shells
- **Beautiful natural grounds** with rock, blueberries and large pine trees dropping cones

## Our Daily Outdoors Routine

### Pre-school Department
We are outdoors for **at least two hours per day**, and more in spring and summer. A dedicated hiking day per week — to Lake Tuvatnet, the wilderness shelter, or the football pitch next door.

### Toddler Department
Set outdoor time in the mornings. We assess weather and season and stay out longer when we can. In very cold weather we have reindeer skins to sit on outdoors, and sometimes we eat breakfast around a campfire.

## Wildlife and Nature

We teach children about the birds and animals around the kindergarten. We may be lucky enough to see:

**Mammals:** Fox · Deer · Squirrel  
**Birds:** Eagle · Blue tit · Great tit · Magpie · Crow · Bullfinch · Ducks · Swans

We have set up a **squirrel feeder** in a tree the pre-school children can see from the window, and we make **bird food** for the trees in our outdoor area. Children learn about sounds, colours, habitat, food and tracks in the snow.

> A roe deer settled at the kindergarten for a period and the children got to pet her and have close contact for a whole week — an experience they will never forget!

## Science Days

Since autumn **2018** we have held **Science Days** where children (mainly pre-school, but younger ones too) take part in experiments and wonder about how the world works. We care about recycling and environmental protection, and think about reuse wherever we can.
