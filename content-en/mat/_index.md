## Food That Nourishes Body and Soul

At Blakstad Nature Kindergarten we place great emphasis on healthy eating habits. Good food is a natural part of daily life, and we want to give children habits that last a lifetime.

## Our Priorities

- **Fruit and vegetables** are an important part of the daily diet
- **Fish and fish products** make up a large part of the menu
- **Healthy alternatives** at celebrations — we use fruit salad and smoothies instead of cake and sweets at birthdays and carnival
- **Clean and simple** — real ingredients rather than processed food

## Our Own Vegetable Garden

The kindergarten has its own vegetable garden where children take part in the whole process — from sowing to harvesting. What we grow varies from year to year, but typically includes:

- **Potatoes**
- **Carrots**
- **Sugar peas**
- **Rhubarb**
- **Apple tree** that gives nice apples in autumn

It is a very special experience for a child to pull up carrots they sowed themselves, or to taste peas picked straight from the plant!

## Campfire Cooking

One of the big favourites is **campfire cooking**. Making food outdoors over an open fire is not only tasty and fun — it teaches children about safety, patience and the joy of making something yourself.

## The Seasons on the Plate

We actively use the seasons in cooking:

- **Summer:** Blueberries picked on the kindergarten grounds, berries from the local area
- **Autumn:** Vegetables from our own garden, mushroom foraging
- **Winter & Spring:** Dried fruit, nuts and nutritious winter food

## Healthy Habits for Life

We believe that good eating habits are formed in childhood. By involving children in growing, harvesting and cooking, we give them a living connection to where food comes from — and a natural love of healthy food.
